The anatomy of a shark

At the front of the shark, you'll find its pointy snout, which it uses to smell out tasty treats from a mile away. Its nose is like a super-powered sniffer, able to detect even the tiniest drops of blood from far, far away.

Right behind the snout, you'll see a row of razor-sharp teeth, each one ready to chomp down on anything that dares to cross the shark's path. And if a tooth happens to fall out, no worries! Sharks have backup teeth ready to take its place.

Moving further down the shark, you'll find its powerful fins, which it uses to zoom through the water like a race car. These fins are like a shark's personal jets, and they help it make tight turns and shoot off in any direction it wants.

And last but not least, the shark's tail is its engine. With a flick of its tail, a shark can go from 0 to 60 in the blink of an eye. It's like having a built-in turbo boost, and it helps the shark catch even the quickest prey.
